About SJTU

Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) is a prestigious public research university located in Shanghai, China. Founded in 1896, it operates under the direct administration of China’s Ministry of Education and has developed a strong reputation for academic excellence, innovation, and global collaboration. SJTU is a member of the elite C9 League, often referred to as China’s equivalent of the Ivy League, and participates in several major national research initiatives.

The university offers a broad range of academic programs across 77 undergraduate disciplines and numerous graduate fields. With modern research facilities, distinguished faculty, and a vibrant international campus environment, SJTU plays a leading role in advancing scientific research, technological development, and global education.

International students from over 150 countries study at SJTU every year.

Vision of the SJTU – Sri Lanka Ambassador Program

The vision of this ambassador initiative is to strengthen awareness and trust toward Shanghai Jiao Tong University among talented students in Sri Lanka. Through structured outreach, accurate information, and authentic student engagement, the program aims to connect high-achieving undergraduate and postgraduate students with world-class academic opportunities at SJTU.

By bridging the information gap between prospective students and the university, this initiative provides guidance on programs, scholarships, and the application process while sharing real experiences from current international students. The goal is not only to increase applications but also to attract motivated and research-oriented students who align with SJTU’s academic excellence and global vision.

Ultimately, this program seeks to position SJTU as a first-choice destination for students in Sri Lanka by building long-term academic connections, mentorship opportunities, and a trusted pathway toward world-class education and research.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need Chinese language to study at SJTU?

Many master's and PhD programs are offered in English. Chinese language is not always required.

What scholarships are available?

The most common scholarships are CSC, SJTU Scholarship, and Shanghai Government Scholarship.

When does the application open?

Applications usually open between December and March depending on the program.

Can I apply without IELTS?

Some programs may accept alternative proof of English proficiency.
